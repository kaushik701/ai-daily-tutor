Day 47: Mastering Re-ranking in LLM Concepts
TL;DR
Re-ranking is a crucial step in information retrieval that involves reordering the initial set of retrieved documents to improve the overall ranking quality.

In plain English

Re-ranking is essentially a two-stage process where the first stage retrieves a large set of possibly relevant documents, and the second stage fine-tunes the ranking of these documents to provide more accurate results.
The first stage is often performed using a retrieval model that can efficiently search through a large corpus of documents.
In the second stage, a more computationally expensive model, such as a cross-encoder, is used to re-rank the top documents retrieved in the first stage, allowing for more precise ranking.

Think of it this way

The process of re-ranking can be thought of as a chef who first selects a set of ingredients based on a recipe, and then carefully arranges and refines the dish to create a culinary masterpiece, where the initial selection is like the first stage of retrieval, and the arrangement and refinement are like the re-ranking stage.

Why it matters for AI Eng interviews

Re-ranking is critical in maximizing the quality of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems, as it directly impacts the accuracy and relevance of the generated responses, which is a key signal in many AI Engineering and ML Engineering job descriptions, particularly those that emphasize the importance of fine-tuning, observability, and production concerns.
One thing to remember
Two-stage retrieval with re-ranking significantly improves the overall ranking quality by allowing for a more precise ranking of the top documents, which is essential for many real-world applications.
