Day 31: Introduction to Large Language Models
TL;DR
A Large Language Model (LLM) is a type of artificial intelligence model designed to process and understand human language at scale, with applications in text generation, conversation, and more.

In plain English

Large Language Models are a type of artificial intelligence designed to understand and generate human-like language. They are trained on vast amounts of text data, which enables them to learn patterns and relationships within language.
The primary function of an LLM is next-token prediction, where the model predicts the next word in a sequence of text given the context of the previous words. This task allows the model to develop a deep understanding of language structure, syntax, and semantics.
As LLMs scale to larger sizes and are trained on more extensive datasets, they often exhibit emergent abilities, such as the capacity to answer questions, translate languages, or even create original content.

Think of it this way

Training a Large Language Model can be thought of as learning to play a game of 'language chess'. Just as a chess player must consider the current state of the board and predict the next move, an LLM must consider the current sequence of words and predict the next word, with the ultimate goal of generating coherent and meaningful text.

Why it matters for AI Eng interviews

Understanding Large Language Models is crucial for AI Engineering and ML Engineering roles, as they are increasingly used in production environments for tasks such as text generation, conversational AI, and language translation. Signals from job descriptions, such as RAG (Retrieve, Augment, Generate) frameworks, agent-based architectures, evaluation metrics, fine-tuning, observability, and Model-Centric Pipelines (MCP), all point to the importance of LLMs in the field, and being able to frame and discuss the entire field of LLMs is essential for success in these roles.
One thing to remember
The ability of Large Language Models to exhibit emergent abilities as they scale is a key factor in their potential to revolutionize numerous applications and industries.
