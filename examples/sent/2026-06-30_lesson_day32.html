Day 32: Autoregressive Generation in LLMs
TL;DR
Autoregressive generation in Large Language Models (LLMs) refers to the process of generating text one token at a time, where each new token is predicted based on the context of the previous tokens.

In plain English

Autoregressive generation is a method used by LLMs to create text. It works by predicting one token at a time, with each prediction being based on the tokens that have come before it. This process is repeated until a stopping criterion is met, such as reaching a maximum sequence length.
The key point about autoregressive generation is that it is sequential. The model cannot generate the next token without knowing the previous ones, which means that the generation process can be slow for long sequences. This is because the model has to make a prediction for each token, one at a time, and it cannot parallelize this process.
This sequential nature of autoregressive generation has significant implications for the efficiency and cost of using LLMs, especially when generating long pieces of text.

Think of it this way

A good analogy for autoregressive generation is a person writing a story one word at a time, where each word is chosen based on the context of the words that have come before it. Just as the writer cannot write the next word without knowing the previous ones, an LLM using autoregressive generation cannot generate the next token without knowing the context of the previous tokens.

Why it matters for AI Eng interviews

Autoregressive generation affects every LLM cost decision because it directly impacts the latency and computational resources required to generate text. This, in turn, affects the cost of using LLMs in production environments, where minimizing latency and computational resources is crucial. As a result, understanding autoregressive generation is essential for AI engineers and ML engineers working with LLMs, as it informs decisions around model architecture, optimization, and deployment strategies, all of which are critical for achieving high performance and low costs in production environments, as highlighted in job descriptions requiring expertise in RAG, model fine-tuning, and production concerns.
One thing to remember
The latency of autoregressive generation scales with the length of the output, making it a critical factor in the design and optimization of LLM-based systems.
