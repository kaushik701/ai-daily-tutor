Day 2: Introduction to Supervised, Unsupervised, and Reinforcement Learning
TL;DR
This lesson covers the basics of supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning, including when to use each type and their applications.

In plain English

Supervised learning involves training a model on labeled data, where the correct output is already known. For example, if we want to build a model that can classify images of dogs and cats, we would use a dataset of images that are already labeled as either 'dog' or 'cat'.
Unsupervised learning, on the other hand, involves training a model on unlabeled data, and the model must find patterns or structure in the data on its own.
Reinforcement learning is a type of learning where an agent learns to take actions in an environment to maximize a reward, such as playing a game or controlling a robot.

Think of it this way

Learning to drive a car can be thought of as a combination of these three types of learning. Initially, we learn from a supervisor (our driving instructor) who provides labeled feedback (e.g., 'turn the wheel to the left'). As we gain experience, we start to learn from our own experiences and mistakes (unsupervised learning), and we learn to take actions to maximize a reward (getting to our destination safely and efficiently), which is similar to reinforcement learning.

Why it matters for AI Eng interviews

Understanding the differences between supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning is crucial for AI engineering roles, as many job descriptions, such as those requiring experience with RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) or agent development, often involve one or more of these types of learning. Additionally, being able to evaluate and fine-tune models, as well as consider production concerns such as model interpretability and observability, can be critical in these roles.
One thing to remember
The choice of learning type depends on the problem you are trying to solve and the type of data you have available.
