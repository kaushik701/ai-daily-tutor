Day 41: Embeddings for Retrieval in AI Engineering
TL;DR
Sentence embeddings enable efficient semantic search by converting text into dense vectors that can be compared using cosine similarity, a crucial concept in AI engineering

In plain English

Embeddings are a way to represent words, sentences, or documents as numbers, called vectors, in a high-dimensional space.
These vectors, or embeddings, are learned during the training process of a model and are designed to capture the semantic meaning of the text.
The goal is to have similar texts mapped to nearby points in this vector space, allowing for efficient comparison and search.

Think of it this way

Imagine a library where each book is represented by a unique location on a map. Books with similar themes or topics are placed close together, making it easy to find related books by looking at the proximity of their locations on the map. This is similar to how sentence embeddings work, where similar sentences are mapped to nearby points in the vector space.

Why it matters for AI Eng interviews

Understanding embeddings for retrieval is critical for AI engineering roles, especially those involving RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) foundations, as it enables the development of efficient and accurate semantic search systems, a key component in many AI applications, including question answering, text summarization, and chatbots.
One thing to remember
The cosine similarity metric is used to compare the similarity between two vectors in the embedding space, with values ranging from -1 (opposite) to 1 (identical), which is essential for evaluating the performance of semantic search systems.
