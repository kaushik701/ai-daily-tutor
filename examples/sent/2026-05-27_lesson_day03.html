Day 3: Training, Validation, and Test Sets in Machine Learning
TL;DR
The division of data into training, validation, and test sets is crucial for evaluating the performance of machine learning models and preventing data leakage.

In plain English

When building a machine learning model, it's essential to split your available data into three parts: training, validation, and test sets. The training set is used to teach the model about the relationships between the data points. The validation set is used to fine-tune the model's parameters and evaluate its performance during the training process. The test set, on the other hand, is used to assess the model's performance on unseen data, providing an unbiased estimate of its accuracy.
Data leakage occurs when information from the test set somehow influences the training process, which can lead to overly optimistic results. This can happen when the model is trained on data that includes information that will not be available during deployment. For instance, if a model is trained to predict stock prices based on historical data that includes future events, it will not perform well when faced with real-world, unseen data.
To avoid data leakage, it's critical to ensure that the data used for training, validation, and testing are properly separated and that no information is shared between them.

Think of it this way

Think of training a machine learning model like training a chef. The training set is like the recipes and cooking techniques the chef learns in culinary school. The validation set is like the practice dishes the chef prepares to refine their skills and adjust their techniques. The test set is like the final exam, where the chef's skills are evaluated in a real-world setting, such as a busy restaurant kitchen, to see how they perform under pressure.

Why it matters for AI Eng interviews

Understanding the importance of training, validation, and test sets is crucial for AI engineering and ML engineering roles, as it directly impacts the model's performance and reliability in production environments. Job descriptions often mention requirements such as model evaluation, fine-tuning, and ensuring the absence of data leakage, highlighting the need for professionals who can properly split and utilize their data to develop robust and accurate models.
One thing to remember
Always keep your training, validation, and test sets separate to prevent data leakage and ensure your model's performance is accurately evaluated.
