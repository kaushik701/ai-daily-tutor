Day 22: Understanding Positional Encoding in Transformers
TL;DR
Positional encoding is a technique used in transformers to preserve the order of input sequences, such as words in a sentence, by adding a fixed vector to the embeddings of each token.

In plain English

Transformers, a type of neural network, have a limitation: they don't inherently understand the order of the input they receive. To address this, positional encoding is used to add information about the position of each token in the sequence.
There are two main types of positional encoding: sinusoidal and learned. Sinusoidal encoding uses a fixed pattern of sine and cosine waves to represent the position of each token, while learned encoding uses a separate neural network to learn the position representations during training.
The choice between sinusoidal and learned encoding depends on the specific task and dataset, with sinusoidal encoding being more commonly used due to its simplicity and effectiveness.

Think of it this way

Consider a recipe where the order of ingredients matters, just like in a sentence where the order of words matters. In cooking, you wouldn't just throw all the ingredients together and expect the dish to turn out right. Similarly, in natural language processing, the order of words is crucial, and positional encoding helps the transformer understand this order, just like following a recipe helps you create the desired dish.

Why it matters for AI Eng interviews

Understanding positional encoding is crucial for AI engineering roles, especially when working with transformer-based models. Job descriptions often mention requirements such as fine-tuning pre-trained models, evaluating model performance, and ensuring observability in production environments. Knowing how transformers handle word order and the trade-offs between sinusoidal and learned positional encoding can help you design and implement more effective models, and can be a key differentiator in interviews, especially when discussing model architecture and performance optimization.
One thing to remember
Positional encoding is essential for transformers to understand the order of input sequences, and the choice between sinusoidal and learned encoding depends on the specific task and dataset.
