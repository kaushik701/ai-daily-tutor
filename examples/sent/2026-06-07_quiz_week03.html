Question 4 · Day 7 · Stochastic vs Batch vs Mini-batch Gradient Descent
What is the main advantage of using Mini-batch Gradient Descent over Stochastic Gradient Descent?
A. Improved computational efficiency
B. Reduced risk of convergence to local minima
C. Increased stability and robustness
D. Enhanced ability to handle large datasets

Answers

Q1: To introduce non-linearity in the model
The ReLU activation function is used to introduce non-linearity in the model, allowing it to learn and represent more complex relationships between the inputs and outputs. This is essential for neural networks to learn and generalize well.
Q2: Reduced risk of overfitting
Random Forests reduce the risk of overfitting by combining the predictions of multiple Decision Trees, which helps to average out the errors and improve the overall performance of the model. This is because Random Forests can handle the variance in the data more effectively.
Q3: The relationship is logistic and probabilistic
Logistic Regression assumes that the relationship between the independent variables and the dependent variable is logistic and probabilistic, meaning that the probability of the dependent variable is modeled using a logistic function. This allows Logistic Regression to predict the probability of a binary outcome.
Q4: Improved computational efficiency
Mini-batch Gradient Descent is more computationally efficient than Stochastic Gradient Descent because it processes multiple examples in parallel, reducing the number of iterations required to converge. This makes it a popular choice for large-scale machine learning tasks.
