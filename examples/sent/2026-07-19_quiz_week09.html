Question 2 · Day 47 · Re-ranking

B. To improve the efficiency of the indexing process
C. To enhance the ranking of documents based on additional relevance signals
D. To reduce the computational cost of the retrieval process
Question 3 · Day 46 · Hybrid Search (BM25 + Dense)
What is the primary advantage of combining BM25 and dense retrieval models in a hybrid search approach?
A. To reduce the computational cost associated with dense retrieval models
B. To leverage the strengths of both sparse and dense representations for improved retrieval effectiveness
C. To simplify the indexing process by using a single representation
D. To enhance the interpretability of the retrieval model by providing feature importance
Question 4 · Day 43 · Approximate Nearest Neighbor Search (HNSW, IVF)
What is the main challenge addressed by approximate nearest neighbor search algorithms like HNSW and IVF?
A. The high computational cost of exact nearest neighbor search in high-dimensional spaces
B. The difficulty in indexing high-dimensional vectors efficiently
C. The need for more accurate retrieval models in search engines
D. The requirement for real-time query processing in streaming data

Answers

Q1: To enhance the relevance of the search results by reformulating the query
Query rewriting aims to rephrase the original query to better capture the user's intent, leading to more accurate and relevant search results. This is particularly important in HyDE, where the query rewriting can significantly impact the effectiveness of the search.
Q2: To enhance the ranking of documents based on additional relevance signals
Re-ranking involves reordering the initially retrieved documents based on additional features or signals that can improve the relevance of the search results. This step can significantly enhance the quality of the search output.
Q3: To leverage the strengths of both sparse and dense representations for improved retrieval effectiveness
Hybrid search models combine the strengths of traditional sparse models like BM25 with the power of dense neural models, aiming to achieve better retrieval performance by leveraging the complementary strengths of both approaches.
Q4: The high computational cost of exact nearest neighbor search in high-dimensional spaces
Approximate nearest neighbor search algorithms like HNSW and IVF are designed to mitigate the high computational cost associated with finding the nearest neighbors in high-dimensional vector spaces, which is a critical operation in many information retrieval and machine learning applications.
