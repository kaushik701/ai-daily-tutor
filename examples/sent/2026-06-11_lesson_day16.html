Day 16: Mastering Batch and Layer Normalization for Stable Training
TL;DR
Batch normalization and layer normalization are techniques used to stabilize and speed up the training of deep neural networks by normalizing the inputs to each layer.

In plain English

Batch normalization and layer normalization are two techniques used to normalize the inputs to each layer in a deep neural network.
Batch normalization normalizes the inputs to each layer across the batch dimension, which means it calculates the mean and standard deviation of the inputs for each feature across all examples in the batch. This helps to reduce the internal covariate shift, which is the change in the distribution of the inputs to each layer during training.
Layer normalization, on the other hand, normalizes the inputs to each layer across all features for each example, which means it calculates the mean and standard deviation of the inputs for each example across all features. This helps to reduce the effect of different scales of the inputs on the training process.

Think of it this way

A good analogy for batch normalization is a cooking recipe where you need to adjust the seasoning of a dish based on the taste of the entire batch, whereas layer normalization is like adjusting the seasoning for each individual dish based on its own taste. This helps to ensure that each dish, or in this case, each example in the batch, is well-seasoned and consistent.

Why it matters for AI Eng interviews

Understanding batch normalization and layer normalization is crucial for AI engineering roles, especially when working with transformer models, as it can significantly impact the stability and performance of the model. Many job descriptions, such as those requiring experience with RAG, agents, and model evaluations, often involve fine-tuning pre-trained models, where normalization techniques play a critical role. Moreover, observability and model interpretability, which are key concerns in production environments, can be improved by applying appropriate normalization techniques.
One thing to remember
Batch normalization is typically used in convolutional neural networks and recurrent neural networks, while layer normalization is commonly used in transformer models and other sequence-to-sequence models.
