Day 46: Mastering Hybrid Search with BM25 and Dense Retrieval
TL;DR
Hybrid search combines the strengths of lexical and semantic search methods to improve the accuracy and relevance of search results.

In plain English

Traditional search methods rely on keyword matching, which can be limited by the user's vocabulary and the quality of the search query.
Hybrid search, on the other hand, uses a combination of lexical and semantic methods to understand the context and intent behind the search query.
By combining these approaches, hybrid search can provide more accurate and relevant results, even when the user's search query is incomplete or ambiguous.

Think of it this way

Combining lexical and semantic search methods is like having a personal shopping assistant who not only understands the keywords you're looking for, but also knows your personal preferences and can suggest related products that you might be interested in, much like a music streaming service recommends songs based on your listening history.

Why it matters for AI Eng interviews

Hybrid search is particularly important for AI Engineering and ML Engineering roles, as it is a key component of production-ready RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) systems, which require the ability to efficiently and effectively search and retrieve relevant information from large datasets, and is closely related to concerns around fine-tuning, observability, and model serving in production environments.
One thing to remember
The key to successful hybrid search is finding the right balance between lexical and semantic methods to achieve optimal search results.
