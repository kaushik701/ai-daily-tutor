Day 49: Fine-tuning vs Prompting vs RAG Decision Framework
TL;DR
This lesson covers the decision framework for choosing between fine-tuning, prompting, and RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) in LLM (Large Language Model) applications.

In plain English

Fine-tuning, prompting, and RAG are three different approaches to adapting LLMs to specific tasks. Fine-tuning involves adjusting the model's weights to fit the new task, prompting involves providing input to guide the model's output, and RAG involves combining the model with a retrieval mechanism to generate more accurate responses.
When deciding between these approaches, consider the amount of data available, the complexity of the task, and the desired level of customization. Fine-tuning is suitable for tasks with large amounts of labeled data, while prompting is better for tasks with limited data or where the model needs to generate text based on a specific input.
RAG is a good choice when the task requires the model to generate text based on specific information or context, and the retrieval mechanism can help to improve the accuracy of the output.

Think of it this way

Choosing between fine-tuning, prompting, and RAG is like deciding how to cook a meal. Fine-tuning is like following a recipe from scratch, where you adjust the ingredients and cooking time to get the desired result. Prompting is like using a pre-made sauce or spice blend, where you provide a starting point and let the model generate the rest. RAG is like having a personal chef who can retrieve ingredients from a pantry and combine them to create a customized dish.

Why it matters for AI Eng interviews

Understanding the decision framework for fine-tuning, prompting, and RAG is crucial for AI engineering roles, as it can impact the performance, efficiency, and scalability of LLM applications. This knowledge is particularly relevant for job descriptions that mention RAG, agents, evaluations, fine-tuning, observability, and production concerns, as it demonstrates the ability to design and optimize LLM systems for real-world applications.
One thing to remember
When choosing between fine-tuning, prompting, and RAG, consider the trade-offs between data requirements, task complexity, and customization level to select the most suitable approach for the specific application.
