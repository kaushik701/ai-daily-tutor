Day 23: Choosing the Right Architecture: BERT vs GPT vs T5
TL;DR
The choice between encoder-only, decoder-only, and encoder-decoder architectures like BERT, GPT, and T5 depends on the specific natural language processing task at hand.

In plain English

Encoder-only architectures, like BERT, are great for tasks that require understanding the input text, such as sentiment analysis or question answering.
Decoder-only architectures, like GPT, are better suited for tasks that require generating text, such as language translation or text summarization.
Encoder-decoder architectures, like T5, can handle both understanding and generating text, making them versatile for a wide range of tasks.

Think of it this way

Think of these architectures like a conversation: BERT is like listening and understanding what someone is saying, GPT is like responding with a generated text, and T5 is like both listening and responding in a conversation.

Why it matters for AI Eng interviews

Choosing the right architecture is crucial in AI Engineering roles, as it directly impacts the performance and efficiency of the model, and is a key consideration in model evaluation, fine-tuning, and production deployment, as highlighted in job descriptions that mention requirements like RAG, agents, and observability.
One thing to remember
The choice of architecture should be driven by the specific requirements of the task, including the type of input and output, and the desired performance metrics.
