Day 12: Introduction to Activation Functions in Deep Learning
TL;DR
Activation functions introduce nonlinearity into neural networks, enabling them to learn and represent complex relationships between inputs and outputs.

In plain English

Activation functions are crucial components of neural networks because they allow the network to learn and represent more complex relationships between inputs and outputs. Without these functions, neural networks would only be able to learn linear relationships, which is a significant limitation for many real-world problems.
The most commonly used activation functions include ReLU (Rectified Linear Unit), GELU (Gaussian Error Linear Unit), sigmoid, and tanh (hyperbolic tangent). Each of these functions has its own strengths and weaknesses, and the choice of which one to use depends on the specific problem being addressed and the architecture of the neural network.
For example, ReLU is widely used in deep neural networks because it is computationally efficient and easy to compute, but it can result in dying neurons, where neurons output zero for all inputs, during training.

Think of it this way

The role of activation functions in neural networks can be thought of like the role of spices in cooking. Just as different spices can add unique flavors to a dish, activation functions add unique properties to a neural network, enabling it to learn and represent complex patterns in data.

Why it matters for AI Eng interviews

Understanding activation functions is essential for AI Engineering and ML Engineering roles, as it directly impacts the performance and interpretability of models, which is a key concern in production environments, especially when it comes to model fine-tuning, observability, and production concerns, such as those related to RAG (Retrieve, Augment, Generate) frameworks, agent-based systems, and model evaluations.
One thing to remember
The choice of activation function can significantly impact the performance of a neural network, so it's essential to understand the strengths and weaknesses of different activation functions and choose the one that best fits the specific problem being addressed.
