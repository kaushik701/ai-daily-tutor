Day 50: Parameter-Efficient Fine-tuning with LoRA and QLoRA
TL;DR
Parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods like LoRA and QLoRA update only a subset of the model's weights, making them more efficient and effective for certain tasks.

In plain English

When fine-tuning large pre-trained models, updating all the weights can be computationally expensive and may lead to overfitting.
Parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods aim to reduce the number of updated weights while still achieving good performance. This is done by adding adapter layers or modifying the existing weights in a way that requires fewer parameters to be updated.
The key idea is to keep the original pre-trained weights fixed and only update the newly introduced parameters, which are typically much fewer in number.

Think of it this way

Think of it like renovating a house. Instead of rebuilding the entire house, you can add a new extension or modify the existing structure to suit your needs. This approach is more efficient and less disruptive, just like how parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods modify the pre-trained model without fully retraining it.

Why it matters for AI Eng interviews

In AI Engineering roles, being familiar with modern fine-tuning standards like LoRA and QLoRA is crucial, as it allows for more efficient and effective model deployment. This is particularly important for production concerns, such as model serving, observability, and evaluation, where computational resources and model performance are critical. Job descriptions often mention requirements like RAG, agents, and fine-tuning, making it essential to understand parameter-efficient methods.
